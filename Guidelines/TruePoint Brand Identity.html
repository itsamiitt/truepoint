01 / Foundation — The Name

Two words for one promise: pointed, and true.

TruePoint pairs accuracy with direction. The product doesn't just gather contacts — it tells you exactly where to aim, and you can trust the bearing. The name is the value proposition in nine letters.

True
verified · accurate · aligned

Data you can stake a quarter on. Verified, calibrated, honest — like a wheel that runs true, or a compass that reads true north.

Point
the lead · the bearing · the apex

The point position out front, the direction to move, and the single best lead to act on now. It's also, quite literally, what the mark does.

True · Point Pointed true. A bearing you can act on without second-guessing. The name avoids hunt-and-predator clichés entirely — this is about precision and trust, not pursuit for its own sake.

03 / The Mark

Three points, rising. The lead one earns the color.

A stack of three chevrons reads as motion, rank and direction at once — a team moving as one, the true point at the apex. That top point carries the single earned accent, so the logo states the whole idea: find the true point. A one-color version is the fallback.

One-color fallback

When the accent can't be reproduced — engraving, fax-grade print, single-ink merch, or a monochrome favicon — every point drops to solid ink. The mark still reads. Never recolor the lower two points; the accent belongs to the apex alone.

04 / Color

Mostly monochrome. Cobalt for brand, Ink for action.

The brand is the product palette. Nearly everything is ink, surface and hairline. Dark Cobalt is the brand accent — it appears as fills only: the mark's apex, the top-score bar, the active state. It is never used as colored text. Ink drives the primary action and all emphasis type.
